Join Lifevs Regular Zara Collection

  • Zara is one of the biggest fast fashion retailers in the world, but its new Join Life collection incorporates sustainable practices to decrease the environmental impact of the clothes
  • It audits factories and creates improvement plans if any of their manufacturers breaches the Code of Conduct
  • The Join Life collection is made from recycled materials, organic cotton, and Tencel lyocell, a wood-based fiber that is manufactured with a closed-circuit process.
  • Each product is not equally sustainably, or sustainable in the same way, so there is a labelling system to tell consumers how each product is sustainable (there is Care For Water, Care For Planet, and Care For Fiber)
  • An estimated 20 to 60 percent of garment production is done by informal workers (or subcontractors hired to fill a certain quota)
  • In competition for lower prices, countries with the lowest production cost prevail at the expense of other countries' industries
  • The clothing industry is the second biggest polluter of clean water
