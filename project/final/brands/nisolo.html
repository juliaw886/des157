Nisolovs Industry

  • Aims to improve the lives and increase professional opportunities of artisans in Latin America
  • Partners with factories in Leon, Mexico to produce their products. They ensure that the factory employees receive health care, paid vacation, and formal employment.
  • 89% of the products come from the Nisolo-owned factory in Trujillo, Peru. Workers receive paid vacation, health care, and skill training in physical education, health, nutrition, English, and yoga classes. They make sure that the shoemakers are able to send their children to school (100% of the shoemakers' children are in school) and have the opportunity to puruse higher education
  • Emphasize workplace diversity in terms of gender. The company is working on equalizing the male-to-female ratio of their employees
  • The company sources leather that are byproducts of the meat industry
  • A typical running shoe releases 30 lbs of carbon dioxide, and most of the emissions stem from the manufacturing process.
  • Most shoes are made in China, where electricity and coal are used frequently to generate steam and run the factory. Discharge from these factories are laced with chemicals and can pollute water sources.
  • In leather shoe production, untreated tannery effluents contain high quantities of heavy metal when left untreated. Things like adhesives, synthetic rubber, and PVC are also environmental hazards when discarded.
