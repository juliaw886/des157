Catch 22 Joseph Heller
Written by World War II veteran Joseph Heller, Catch 22 details the war from the perspective of protagonist Army Air Corps Captain John Yossarian. The book is unapologetically satirical, ditching all pretense and heroism in favor of a critical look at the war and the people fighting in it. For one, Yossarian spends the entire book thinking of ways to be discharged from combat. He pretends to be sick multiple times, begs the army doctor to forge medical papers, and eventually *SPOILER ALERT* decides to run away to Sweden to escape the war altogether. Catch 22: A requirement that cannot be met until a prerequisite is met. However, the prerequisite cannot be obtained until the original requirement is met. - Urban Dictionary

The Luminaries Eleanor Catton A murder mystery set in the time of the New Zealand gold rush, the book details the events of one specific day from the perspective of twelve different people. It keeps a quick pace throughout, never losing steam until the very end and making it a difficult book to put down. “Luck only happens once and it's always an accident when it does.” - The Luminaries
