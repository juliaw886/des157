Pocket Full of Books

Catch 22 Joseph Heller
Catch 22 details WWII from the perspective of protagonist Army Air Corps Captain John Yossarian. The book is unapologetically satirical, ditching all pretense and heroism in favor of a critical look at the war and the people fighting in it. For one, Yossarian spends the entire book thinking of ways to be discharged from combat. He pretends to be sick multiple times, begs the army doctor to forge medical papers, and eventually *SPOILER ALERT* decides to run away to Sweden to escape the war altogether. Catch 22: A requirement that cannot be met until a prerequisite is met. However, the prerequisite cannot be obtained until the original requirement is met. - Urban Dictionary

The Luminaries Eleanor Catton A murder mystery set in the time of the New Zealand gold rush, the book details the events of one specific day from the perspective of twelve different people. It keeps a quick pace throughout, never losing steam until the very end and making it a difficult book to put down. “Luck only happens once and it's always an accident when it does.” - The Luminaries

Hotel Pastis Peter Mayle I haven't actually read this book yet, but it has a really nice cover. It's about a bored British ad-man who escapes to France in the "Eat, Pray, Love" style. But with a murder mystery and shadier characters. Unfortunately, after googling this book for this assignment and reading some reviews, I'm a little less inclined to read it. “There is something cantankerous and grasping, as well as dull, about this book.” - The Independent UK Review

Paper Girls Brian K. Vaughan This comic book series was sent to me by a friend and, while I haven't gotten around to it yet, seems like a really interesting read. There's something about the colors and illustrations that screams 80's to me. Written by a former Lost writer and author of a slew of famous Marvel franchises, Paper Girls follows a band of teenagers who get sucked into a fight to save the world. “I'm not going to stand here and be eaten by some b****'s dinosaur. I am finally doing something with my life.” - Paper Girls
